The Stakes

Why Informatics Matters

Every year, medical errors kill more people than car accidents. Most aren't caused by bad doctors — they're caused by bad information flow. A missed allergy note. A lab result stuck in a fax queue. A drug interaction buried in a 200-page paper chart. Clinical informatics is the discipline that fixes this.

❌ Without Informatics

Paper charts lost between departments
Allergies handwritten — missed by new doctor
Lab results faxed 3 days later
Drug interactions caught after administration
Billing errors cost hospitals $36B/year

✔ With Informatics

Single digital record, accessible anywhere
Allergy alerts fire in real-time at point of care
Lab results appear on screen in seconds
AI catches drug interactions before prescribing
Automated coding reduces claim denials by 30%

Scene 02
Data Acquisition · RxNorm · SNOMED CT

The Smart
Check-In

Instead of a clipboard, Alex is handed a tablet. When they select "Advil," the system doesn't just record the word — it encodes the specific RxNormRxNorm is a standardized nomenclature for clinical drugs. Each drug concept receives a unique identifier, enabling interoperability across pharmacy, EHR, and lab systems. code (5640), so every computer in the network knows exactly what molecule is in Alex's body. This is the death of the paper record.

🔬 Informatics Spotlight

This is Structured Data Acquisition. Unstructured text like "Advil" is meaningless to a machine. RxNorm provides a universal code so a lab robot in the basement and a pharmacist in Sacramento speak the same language.

RxNorm SNOMED CT EHR Sync

AI Logic

The system uses auto-complete with coded suggestions — as Alex types "Ad..." it surfaces "Advil (Ibuprofen 200mg · RxNorm 5640)" rather than free text, ensuring 100% structured capture.

Scene 04
HL7 FHIR · LOINC · SNOMED CT · Interoperability

The Hidden
Language

Alex gets a blood test. The vial goes to the basement lab. For the result to automatically appear on Dr. Garcia's screen — with no fax, no phone call, no delay — the lab machine and the EHR must speak the exact same language. That universal language is HL7 FHIRFast Healthcare Interoperability Resources (FHIR) is a modern standard for exchanging healthcare data electronically. It uses RESTful APIs and JSON/XML to make health data accessible and portable..

🔬 Informatics Spotlight

LOINC gives the test a universal name (like a part number). SNOMED CT codes the clinical finding. HL7 FHIR is the "USB plug" — the transport protocol that moves the data between any two systems in a standard format.

HL7 FHIR R4 LOINC SNOMED CT Interoperability

AI Logic

FHIR's API-first design means AI models can directly query patient records from any system. This is what powers third-party health apps — with patient consent — to pull data from any FHIR-compliant EHR.

Scene 05
Data Warehouse · CDR · Predictive AI · Learning Health System

The Vault

Alex goes home. But their data starts its second life. A Clinical Data RepositoryA CDR stores real-time clinical data from the EHR for immediate patient care. Unlike a data warehouse, it contains identifiable, live data used by clinicians. (CDR) keeps the live record for ongoing care. A Data Warehouse stores a de-identified copy alongside thousands of other patients. An AI model running overnight notices that Alex's lab values match a historical pattern seen in pre-sepsis patients — and sends Dr. Garcia a proactive alert the next morning.

🔬 Informatics Spotlight

The Learning Health System is a loop: patient care generates data → data trains AI → AI improves care → better outcomes generate better data. UC Davis Health's system, PREDICTOR, continuously mines the warehouse to catch deteriorating patients.

CDR Data Warehouse Learning Health System

AI Logic

Predictive Analytics models (often XGBoost or neural nets) trained on millions of de-identified records can identify patients at risk for sepsis, readmission, or deterioration before a clinician might notice.

Scene 06
Revenue Cycle · ICD-10 · CPT · Medical Coding · EDI

The Paperwork

Two weeks later, the hospital needs to get paid. A medical coder (or AI) translates Dr. Garcia's note into two types of codes: ICD-10International Classification of Diseases, 10th Revision. Contains 70,000+ diagnosis codes used worldwide for billing, epidemiology, and clinical research. for the diagnosis (what Alex had) and CPT for the service (what was done). These codes are bundled into an electronic claim and transmitted to the insurer via EDI X12. If informatics is done right — it's paid automatically. If not, a human steps in.

🔬 Informatics Spotlight

ICD-10-CM has over 0+ diagnosis codes. CPT codes (maintained by the AMA) describe every billable medical service. Accurate coding is crucial — hospitals lose billions annually to coding errors or under-documentation.

ICD-10-CM CPT Codes Revenue Cycle EDI X12 837P

AI Logic

AI-assisted coding (like 3M CDI or Nuance AI) reads the physician's note, suggests ICD-10/CPT codes with confidence scores, and flags cases where documentation is insufficient before the claim is submitted.

Scene 07
Telehealth · RPM · Wearables · Patient-Generated Data

The Remote
Lifeline

Three weeks after Alex's visit, Dr. Garcia schedules a telehealth follow-up. But the real innovation isn't the video call — it's what happens between visits. Alex's smartwatch continuously streams heart rate, blood oxygen, and sleep data to the EHR via Remote Patient MonitoringRPM uses digital technologies to collect health data from patients in one location and electronically transmit it to providers in a different location for assessment and recommendations. (RPM). When Alex's resting heart rate spikes to 105 bpm at 3 AM, the system flags it before the next appointment.

🔬 Informatics Spotlight

Patient-Generated Health Data (PGHD) from wearables and IoT devices is transforming care from episodic to continuous. The challenge: most PGHD is unstructured. FHIR's Observation resource type standardizes how wearable data integrates with the EHR.

RPM PGHD IoT Health Telehealth

AI Logic

Anomaly detection algorithms continuously analyze streaming wearable data against Alex's personal baseline. A heart rate of 105 might be normal during exercise, but at 3 AM during sleep, the AI knows to escalate.

Scene 08
HIPAA · De-identification · Audit Trails · Zero Trust

The Guardian

Every click, every view, every access to Alex's record is logged. HIPAAThe Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (1996) sets national standards for protecting sensitive patient health information from disclosure without patient consent. mandates that only authorized personnel can view Protected Health Information (PHI). When a curious employee from a different department tries to peek at Alex's record, the system blocks the attempt and alerts compliance — all within seconds.

🔬 Informatics Spotlight

Role-Based Access Control (RBAC) ensures that a billing clerk sees only billing data, a nurse sees clinical data, and nobody sees what they don't need. De-identification (Safe Harbor or Expert Determination) strips 18 identifiers before data enters the research warehouse.

HIPAA RBAC PHI De-identification Audit Trail

AI Logic

Behavioral analytics AI monitors access patterns. If a staff member suddenly accesses 50 records in an hour (vs. their usual 5), the system flags anomalous behavior for investigation — a principle called Zero Trust.

Scene 09
Population Health · SDOH · Surveillance · Registries

Population
Health

Alex is one patient. But zoom out, and their data — combined with millions of others — reveals patterns invisible at the individual level. Population health informaticsThe use of information technology to collect, analyze, and act on health data at the population level to improve public health outcomes and reduce disparities. transforms clinical data into public health intelligence: which zip codes have the highest diabetes rates? Where should UC Davis deploy mobile clinics? Which Social Determinants of Health (SDOH) most predict readmission?

🔬 Informatics Spotlight

Disease registries (like cancer registries mandated by law) track outcomes across populations. Syndromic surveillance systems monitor ER chief complaints in real-time to detect outbreaks before lab confirmation arrives — the digital equivalent of a canary in a coal mine.

Population Health SDOH Syndromic Surveillance Disease Registry

AI Logic

Geospatial AI combines clinical data with census, environmental, and socioeconomic data to create risk maps. UC Davis uses these models to identify health deserts and allocate resources where they're needed most.

Scene 10
Genomics · Precision Medicine · Digital Twins · LLMs

The Future

What if informatics could predict Alex's response to a drug before it's prescribed? PharmacogenomicsThe study of how genes affect a person's response to drugs. Combines pharmacology and genomics to develop effective, safe medications tailored to a person's genetic makeup. reads Alex's DNA to find that they're a poor metabolizer of certain drugs. A digital twin — a computational model of Alex's physiology — simulates treatments in silico. And Large Language Models synthesize Alex's entire medical history into actionable clinical summaries in seconds.

🔬 Informatics Spotlight

CPIC guidelines (Clinical Pharmacogenetics Implementation Consortium) translate genomic test results into actionable prescribing decisions. UC Davis is a leader in implementing pharmacogenomic CDS alerts directly into Epic.

Pharmacogenomics Digital Twins LLMs Precision Medicine

AI Logic

Foundation models trained on biomedical literature, clinical notes, and genomic data can reason across modalities. The key challenge: hallucination guardrails and source attribution to ensure AI recommendations are grounded in evidence.
