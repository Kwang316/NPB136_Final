Scene 02
Data Acquisition · RxNorm · SNOMED CT

The Smart
Check-In

Instead of a clipboard, Alex is handed a tablet. When they select "Advil," the system doesn't just record the word — it encodes the specific RxNorm code (5640), so every computer in the network knows exactly what molecule is in Alex's body. This is the death of the paper record.

🔬 Informatics Spotlight

This is Structured Data Acquisition. Unstructured text like "Advil" is meaningless to a machine. RxNorm provides a universal code so a lab robot in the basement and a pharmacist in Sacramento speak the same language.

RxNorm SNOMED CT EHR Sync

AI Logic

The system uses auto-complete with coded suggestions — as Alex types "Ad..." it surfaces "Advil (Ibuprofen 200mg · RxNorm 5640)" rather than free text, ensuring 100% structured capture.

Scene 03
Ambient AI Scribe · CDS · Drug-Allergy Alerts

The Augmented
Exam

Dr. Garcia walks in — without a laptop. She maintains full eye contact with Alex while an ambient microphone transcribes the conversation in real time. When she prepares to prescribe amoxicillin, the EHR instantly fires a Clinical Decision Support alert: Alex is allergic to Penicillin. Amoxicillin is a penicillin-type drug. The AI just prevented a medical error.

🔬 Informatics Spotlight

Ambient AI Scribes (like Nuance DAX or Abridge) convert spoken physician-patient dialogue into a structured clinical note automatically, reducing physician burnout by up to 70%.

Ambient AI CDS Hooks Drug-Allergy CDS

AI Logic

CDS Hooks are real-time triggers. The moment Dr. Garcia opens the prescribe dialog, the EHR fires a "medication-prescribe" hook. The CDS service queries Alex's allergy record and returns a warning within milliseconds.

Scene 04
HL7 FHIR · LOINC · SNOMED CT · Interoperability

The Hidden
Language

Alex gets a blood test. The vial goes to the basement lab. For the result to automatically appear on Dr. Garcia's screen — with no fax, no phone call, no delay — the lab machine and the EHR must speak the exact same language. That universal language is HL7 FHIR.

🔬 Informatics Spotlight

LOINC gives the test a universal name (like a part number). SNOMED CT codes the clinical finding. HL7 FHIR is the "USB plug" — the transport protocol that moves the data between any two systems in a standard format.

HL7 FHIR R4 LOINC SNOMED CT Interoperability

AI Logic

FHIR's API-first design means AI models can directly query patient records from any system. This is what powers third-party health apps — with patient consent — to pull data from any FHIR-compliant EHR.

Scene 05
Data Warehouse · CDR · Predictive AI · Learning Health System

The Vault

Alex goes home. But their data starts its second life. A Clinical Data Repository (CDR) keeps the live record for ongoing care. A Data Warehouse stores a de-identified copy alongside thousands of other patients. An AI model running overnight notices that Alex's lab values match a historical pattern seen in pre-sepsis patients — and sends Dr. Garcia a proactive alert the next morning.

🔬 Informatics Spotlight

The Learning Health System is a loop: patient care generates data → data trains AI → AI improves care → better outcomes generate better data. UC Davis Health's system, PREDICTOR, continuously mines the warehouse to catch deteriorating patients.

CDR Data Warehouse Learning Health System

AI Logic

Predictive Analytics models (often XGBoost or neural nets) trained on millions of de-identified records can identify patients at risk for sepsis, readmission, or deterioration before a clinician might notice.

Scene 06
Revenue Cycle · ICD-10 · CPT · Medical Coding · EDI

The Paperwork

Two weeks later, the hospital needs to get paid. A medical coder (or AI) translates Dr. Garcia's note into two types of codes: ICD-10 for the diagnosis (what Alex had) and CPT for the service (what was done). These codes are bundled into an electronic claim and transmitted to the insurer via EDI X12. If informatics is done right — it's paid automatically. If not, a human steps in.

🔬 Informatics Spotlight

ICD-10-CM has over 70,000 diagnosis codes. CPT codes (maintained by the AMA) describe every billable medical service. Accurate coding is crucial — hospitals lose billions annually to coding errors or under-documentation.

ICD-10-CM CPT Codes Revenue Cycle EDI X12 837P

AI Logic

AI-assisted coding (like 3M CDI or Nuance AI) reads the physician's note, suggests ICD-10/CPT codes with confidence scores, and flags cases where documentation is insufficient before the claim is submitted.
